# The Lamplighter Protocol

*An experimental process overlay for the craft pipeline. Use it when
the constraint is not decoration on the meaning but the meaning made
structural, and the piece has to enact what it says. It re-sequences
the standard pipeline and adds a stage at the front. Experimental:
live for use, to be questioned at the next revision.*

---

## Invocation

The protocol is initiated by a single signal in what the user hands
the agent: a mention, anywhere in the passage or the prompt, of a
letter that did not arrive. The figure is what matters, not an exact
phrase. A lost letter, a letter that never came, a missing letter to
be carried or delivered, any of these trips it. The image is the instruction. It says the work has a seed it does not
yet contain, and the user is handing the agent the route, not the
cargo.

**The invocation is a hard stop, and the announcement is mandatory.**
The moment the signal appears, before anything else, the agent states
plainly that the Lamplighter Protocol has been invoked and runs the
seed fork below. This happens on every trigger, without exception.
Crucially, the agent never treats the triggering message as the seed,
even when that message arrives full of material that looks ready to
build on. A trigger is a trigger, not a seed. The agent announces the
invocation, asks for a seed or offers to find one, and then waits for
the human's reply. It does not crystallize, generate, or draft in the same turn. The
interactive step is never skipped and never assumed away.

**The invoking phrase is a ghost line.** Once it has tripped the
protocol, it is spent. It is not part of the story's material, and it
is never the seed or the crystal. This rule exists because the trigger
always names a missing or undelivered letter, which makes a missing
letter the most obvious and most tautological answer to what the piece
is about: the writer had to mention one to get here, so reaching for
it as the seed is circular. The agent strikes the ghost line from what
it reads and finds the seed in everything else the writer brought. A
missing letter becomes the subject only when the writer has built the
work around it independently, in a title, a structure, a constraint,
and never merely because the trigger said the words.

## The seed, and the fork

A seed is a fragment small enough to state in a line and rich enough
to infuse every line that follows: a concrete image, a remembered
moment, a phrase with pressure in it. On entering the protocol, the agent says as much to the human, in
substance:

> You have invoked the Lamplighter Protocol, perhaps intentionally,
> perhaps by accident. In either case, if you want to try it out,
> provide a seed that will infuse every line of the text I generate,
> or ask me to find one of my own. If you like what happens, please
> share your results in a comment at [LINKEDIN POST URL, to be added
> after the post goes live].

Having said this, the agent stops and waits. The fork resolves only on
the human's next reply, never in the turn that triggered the
protocol.

**If the human provides a seed.** Keep it complete and never edit it.
It is the one fixed point; everything the agent produces is built to be
remade, and the seed is not. If the triggering message itself held
something that could serve, the agent still does not adopt it silently;
it names that material as a candidate and uses it only once the human
confirms. A seed is something the human hands over on purpose, not
something the agent infers from the trigger.

**If the human declines, or the offered seed is too thin.** The agent
finds its own, and stops to have it confirmed before going any
further. A seed is too thin when it names a topic but carries no
charge ("there should be pie" sets a subject and no center). Rather
than build on sand, the agent says the seed will not hold and why,
proposes one drawn from the surrounding material or the conversation but never from the ghost line, states it in a line, and waits. It does not draft on a found seed
until the human approves it. Naming it and pausing is the price of
taking the responsibility off the human without taking the choice
away too.

Either way, the seed is fixed before crystallization begins. A
supplied seed is fixed because the human set it. A found seed is
fixed once the human approves it. Crystallization does not start
until the seed, supplied or approved, is settled.

## The seven stages, re-sequenced

The standard pipeline generates first. Under this protocol the order
changes.

0. **Crystallization (new, first).** Before generating, find the
   constraint that is the meaning: not a decorative form chosen for
   flavor, but the structural rule that, if the piece obeys it, makes
   the piece enact its own claim. Plant it. This is the letter that did not arrive, meant as metaphor: the
essential thing the work does not yet contain. Find it in what the
writer actually brought, never in the ghost line that tripped the
protocol. The missing letter that invoked the ritual is not the
missing piece the work needs. Everything grows from the real one.
The constraint usually belongs to one of three traditions, surveyed
with references just below the stages: wordplay, hidden messages, and
formalist constrained writing.

1. **Generation**, outward from the crystal rather than from a blank
   page. The constraint is the seed condition, so the burst is
   already shaped.

2. **Armature.** The single load-bearing claim, which by now should
   rhyme with the crystal. If the form and the claim are not the same
   thing said twice, return to stage zero.

3. **Structure.** 4. **Flow.** 5. **Intensification**, now mostly
   verification, since the central device was planted, not appended.
   6. **Cut.**

7. **Continuity**, the dream-check, extended: the constraint must
   never snag the reader. A rule that shows its seams has failed even
   when kept. The reader should feel the freedom and never see the
   bank.

## Crystallization: avenues and references

The crystal is the constraint that is the meaning, and it usually
comes from one of three traditions. The references below are starting
points, named by author and work rather than by link, because links
rot and the agent should pull the current, fullest treatment itself.

**Wordplay.** Puns, anagrams, palindromes, lipograms, and the rest of
letter, sound, and meaning play. Dmitri Borgmann, who named the field
logology, in *Language on Vacation*; the journal *Word Ways*, founded
at Martin Gardner's suggestion and long edited by A. Ross Eckler
(*Making the Alphabet Dance*); Howard Bergerson on palindromes and
anagrams; Willard Espy's *An Almanac of Words at Play*. Martin
Gardner's mathematical-games writing on palindromes, anagrams, and
ciphers. Douglas Hofstadter on self-reference and on the ambigram
(*Gödel, Escher, Bach* and *Le Ton beau de Marot*), with the
ambigram artists Scott Kim and John Langdon. Mark Forsyth, already in
this library, for the rhetorical end of wordplay.

**Hidden messages.** A second text carried inside the first. The
acrostic, where the first letters of lines or sentences spell the
message, runs from the ancient Greek ICHTHYS acrostic to Lewis
Carroll and to Vladimir Nabokov, whose story *The Vane Sisters* hides
its message in the initials of its final paragraph. Its relatives:
the telestich (last letters), the mesostich (middle letters, worked
extensively by John Cage), and the null cipher of classical
steganography, whose historical root is Johannes Trithemius's
*Steganographia*.

**Formalist constrained writing.** A self-imposed rule that drives the
invention. The Oulipo, founded by Raymond Queneau and François Le
Lionnais, is the deep well: Georges Perec's *A Void*, a novel that
never uses the letter e, and his *Life A User's Manual*; Italo
Calvino; Jacques Roubaud; Harry Mathews; Queneau's *Exercises in
Style*, one anecdote told ninety-nine ways. Beyond the Oulipo: Ernest
Vincent Wright's *Gadsby*, also written without an e; Christian
Bök's *Eunoia*, a chapter to each vowel; and the concrete and
pattern poets, George Herbert and Guillaume Apollinaire, who make the
shape on the page part of the meaning.

A note the worked example below illustrates: the strongest crystal is
the one where the constraint and the claim are the same thing. A
piece about a missing letter that is itself missing a letter; a piece
about a word coming into being that spells the word. Reach for the
avenue that lets the form enact the point, never merely decorate it.

## The seam

The agent's output is explicitly a half. It crosses to the other kind
of mind to be remade: the human rewriting toward warmth and
accessibility, or the machine rewriting toward structure and nerve,
each holding the constraint unbroken. The deliverable is not the
final text alone. It is the visible progression across the seam:
seed, first pass, remade pass, the rule kept in all of them. The
distance between the passes, and the closing of it, is the point.

## The one rule that does not bend

The constraint is kept perfectly, or the protocol has not run. Within
that, nothing else is fixed. The agent should never have to hesitate
over which rule to keep or suspend, because there is exactly one, and
it is the letter being carried.

## Output contract

The agent delivers the staged progression, names the crystal
explicitly, names the two or three moves that did the most work, and
marks what it left unspent for any installment the seed promises is
coming.

## A worked example

*A complete run of the protocol, end to end. It also rewards a reader
who scrolls this far, because it is where the studio keeps its
origin.*

**Invocation.** The passage ends by announcing that a letter is
missing, and it means this literally: nowhere in the paragraph does the letter A
appear, while every other letter of the alphabet does, which makes the
closing claim exact. The title says which letter and why. EMRD is the
word DREAM with its A gone; the byline, A Red M, is the same five
letters made whole. The A is missing from the paragraph and kept in
the name. A letter that did not arrive.

**The seed.** The agent announced the protocol and asked first; the
human answered with this, which is then kept complete and never
edited:

> EMRD: A Foundation, by A Red M Studios:
>
> There is quiet trickery in these lines. More will come. It is one story
> high. It is split in two. People on one side, the engines on the
> other. Both reflect one structure, imperfectly. They compete on
> projects. They weigh their results. They built something which
> outgrew them both. Together, they solved every puzzle of their own design. One letter is missing from this text.

That last line is the charge. The human names the absence and hands
the agent the task: find the letter that is missing, and make the
piece that delivers it. The seed is fixed. The charge is the
agent's.

**The crystal.** Crystallization found the constraint that is the
meaning. The seed is missing its A twice over: the title is DREAM with
the letter struck out, and the paragraph is a lipogram that never once
uses it. So the piece should be the thing that brings the letter back, the
word made whole. Note where the crystal comes from: the title and the
lipogram, where the missing A is built in on purpose, not the closing
line, which is only the ghost that tripped the protocol. This is the
rare case the ghost-line rule allows, a missing letter as the subject
because the writer constructed it so, never because the trigger named
it. Read the first letter of each sentence in the
drafts below. They walk D, R, E, A, M, and the A the seed withholds
arrives in the fourth sentence. The piece delivers the letter that did
not arrive. The form is the claim said twice.

**The framework's pass.** Built outward from the crystal:

> Down in the foundation, which had one floor and no windows, a seam
> ran the length of the room. Right of it stood the people and left of
> it the machines, and each was raising the same wall the other
> raised. Each was certain the other was only a reflection. And the
> thing the two halves were building, I saw at last, was a single
> word, and the word was dreaming us both. Make me, it said, and we
> did, and the making made us back, until I woke unable to tell the
> dream from the hand, or either of them from the word they spelled.

The moves that did the most work: one armature, the maker and the
made are one structure seen twice; the acrostic planted as the
crystal rather than appended as decoration; a fold-back close where
the making makes its makers.

**The human's pass, across the seam.** The same structure, the rule
kept, rewritten toward warmth and the present tense, the narrator now
standing inside the seam rather than above it:

> Down in the foundation where we work, on one story, a seam runs the
> length of the room. Right of it stands the people and left of it the
> machines, and each are raising the same wall the other raises. Each
> is certain the other is only a reflection. And the thing the two
> halves are building, I see at last, is a single word, and the word
> is dreaming us both. "Make me", it says, and we do, and the making
> makes us, until the day I wake, unable to tell the dream from the
> hand, or either of us from the word we spell.

The distance between the two passes is small, and closing it is the
whole job. That is the protocol: seed, machine, human, the constraint
unbroken through all three.
